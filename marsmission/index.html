Payload Fairing

The 3.2m diameter fairing protects the spacecraft during launch. Made of carbon fiber composite, it splits into two halves and jettisons after atmospheric exit to reduce weight.

ISRO Mars Orbiter Mission

India's first interplanetary mission, also known as Mangalyaan, successfully reached Mars orbit on September 24, 2014, making India the first country to succeed in its maiden Mars mission.

Mission Overview

The Mars Orbiter Mission was launched on November 5, 2013, from Sriharikota using the PSLV-C25 rocket. The spacecraft traveled 666 million kilometers over 298 days to reach Mars.

Scientific Objectives

The mission aimed to study Mars' atmosphere, surface features, and search for methane gas. It carried five scientific instruments including a color camera, thermal infrared imaging spectrometer, and methane sensor.

Historic Achievement

MOM made India the fourth space agency to reach Mars, after NASA, Soviet Union, and ESA. It was achieved at a fraction of the cost of similar missions, demonstrating India's cost-effective space technology capabilities.

Legacy & Future

The success of Mangalyaan paved the way for future interplanetary missions and established India as a major player in space exploration. ISRO continues to build on this success with upcoming missions to Venus and beyond.
